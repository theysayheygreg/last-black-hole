## 1. Context and the World Record
The video focuses on a sub-25-minute run, explaining that *Celeste* is a premier speedrunning title due to its robust in-game timer. The timer pauses during level transitions and cutscenes, allowing runners to maintain focus without being penalized for technical load times. The run is framed not just as a platforming challenge, but as a feat of physics manipulation.

## 2. Momentum Conservation
The core of *Celeste* speedrunning is exploiting the game's momentum system. While casual play involves simple dashes, speedrunners use **Momentum Triggers**:
*   **Inherited Velocity:** If a player jumps while a dash is active, the game "inherits" the dash's velocity and adds it to the jump.
*   **Super Dash:** A horizontal dash followed by a jump, providing high verticality and fast horizontal speed.
*   **Hyper Dash:** A down-diagonal dash followed by a jump, resulting in a low but extremely fast horizontal burst.

## 3. The "Extended" Hyper & Wave Dash
High-level movement relies on the **10-Frame Rule**:
*   **The Mechanic:** If Madeline (the character) stays on the ground for at least 10 frames during a dash, her dash charge resets.
*   **Extended Hyper:** By jumping *after* these 10 frames but *before* the dash momentum ends, a runner keeps the massive speed while **regaining their dash charge mid-air**.
*   **Wave Dashing:** An "Extended Hyper from the air." By dashing diagonally down into the ground and jumping immediately upon impact, runners can chain infinite high-speed movements across flat terrain.

## 4. Advanced "Insane" Techniques
The video breaks down "broken" mechanics that allow runners to bypass intended level design:
*   **Ultras:** Dashing down-diagonally while already moving at high speed (e.g., after a Wave Dash). If the character hits the ground after the dash ends, they receive a **1.2x speed multiplier**. These can be chained exponentially.
*   **Corner Boosting:** Jumping off the very top edge of a wall while holding "grab." This requires frame-perfect timing and adds a flat +40 to horizontal speed.
*   **Wall Bouncing:** Dashing vertically into a wall and jumping at the right moment to convert upward dash momentum into a massive vertical leap.
*   **The Demo Dash (Crouch Dash):** By dashing and crouching on the same frame, Madeline’s hitbox shrinks. This allows runners to dash through 4-pixel gaps in spikes or "dust bunnies" that were never intended to be passable.

## 5. Developer "Forgiveness" (Coyote Time)
The developers included "feel-good" mechanics to make the game more accessible, which speedrunners repurposed as precision tools:
*   **Coyote Time:** A 5-frame window that allows jumping after walking off a ledge.
*   **Jump Buffering:** Registering a jump input up to 4 frames before touching the ground.
Runners use these windows as precise timing anchors for executing advanced tech.

## 6. Optimization: Control Layouts
To perform these frame-perfect tasks, top-level runners often use **multiple jump buttons** (up to three). This allows for rapid-fire inputs, such as "double corner boosts," that would be physically impossible on a single button.

## 7. The Result: Breaking the Game
The combination of these techniques allows runners to clear rooms in seconds that were designed to take half a minute. Platforming challenges are transformed into a "dance" of physics exploits, where the runner effectively ignores the environment's constraints to reach the summit in record time.
